        “Nitrate in drinking water is a contaminant which can affect
        human health and has been associated with an increased risk
        of, amongst other diseases, colorectal cancer. [...] The
        analysis shows that a lower limit of 9.25 mg/L would decrease
        the annual number of colorectal cancer cases by 72 [...]
        and by an additional 55 \% [...] for a stricter limit of 3.87

        \begin{itemize}
            \item Nitrate prevents oxygen from binding to their blood.
                Can progress to cause coma and death if not left
                untreated~\cite{knobeloch_blue_2000,
                chen_methemoglobinemia_2026}.

            \item Water used in formula contained nitrate-nitrogen
                concentrations of 22.9 and 27.4 mg/L~\cite{knobeloch_blue_2000}.

        \end{itemize}
